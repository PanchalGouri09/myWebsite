Situation Overview

The world didn't stumble into this war —
it was dragged in, eyes open.

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched surprise airstrikes across Iran — assassinating Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in the opening salvo. The world has not been the same since.

What began as a targeted military campaign has spiraled into a war of attrition stretching across the entire Middle East. Iran, far from collapsing, has fought back — closing the Strait of Hormuz, striking US allies across nine countries, and elevating a hardliner as its new Supreme Leader.


Meanwhile, in January 2026, the US had already made a dramatic move in the Western Hemisphere — capturing Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro. The message to the world was unmistakable.

Chronology of Conflict

How We
Got Here

June 2025
🇮🇱 🇺🇸 The Twelve-Day War

Israel & US Strike Iran's Nuclear Sites — The "12-Day War"

In a surprise attack, Israel bombs Iran's nuclear and military facilities, assassinating Revolutionary Guard commanders and nuclear scientists. Iran retaliates with 550+ ballistic missiles and 1,000 drones. The US intercepts attacks and bombs three nuclear sites. After 12 days, a US-brokered ceasefire takes hold — but the peace is fragile.

Jan 2026
🇺🇸 🇻🇪 Venezuela Gambit

"The Maduro Raid" — US Seizes Venezuelan President

In a stunning show of force, the United States captures Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. Trump declares the US will "take back" Venezuela's oil. The operation severs Iran-Venezuela economic ties — both nations had used each other to bypass sanctions. Iran sees the writing on the wall.

Jan-Feb 2026
🇮🇷 Iran Boils Over

Mass Protests Erupt — Iran's Streets on Fire

Beginning December 28, 2025, nationwide protests triggered by economic collapse and 60% inflation spread to all 31 Iranian provinces. The regime violently suppresses them — killing thousands. Trump threatens military action. The situation becomes a powder keg, even as nuclear negotiations appear to near a breakthrough.

Feb 25, 2026
🕊️ Diplomacy's Last Gasp

"A Historic Agreement Is Within Reach" — Then Silence

Iran's Foreign Minister announces a deal is "within reach." Diplomacy appears to be working. Three days later, the bombs fall. The US and Israel never intended to wait.

Feb 28, 2026
💥 OPERATION EPIC FURY — DAY ONE

The US & Israel Launch — Supreme Leader Khamenei Killed

Surprise joint airstrikes at dawn. The opening salvo assassinates Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei along with senior family members and officials. Tehran becomes a ghost town. Cities across Iran brace for sustained bombardment. The Strait of Hormuz becomes a warzone. The world's oil supply hangs in the balance.

Mar 2026
🌊 The Escalation Spiral

Iran Strikes 9 Countries — Hormuz Choked, Oil Hits $100+

Unlike 2025, Iran expands the battlefield — launching missiles and drones at US assets and civilian infrastructure across Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, and the UAE. Nearly 2,000 distinct military events documented. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 19% of global LNG passes — is effectively closed. Brent crude exceeds $100/barrel.

Mar 17, 2026
⚡ TODAY — No End in Sight

Day 17: A War of Attrition With No Off-Ramp

The International Crisis Group calls for an immediate ceasefire. The UN World Food Programme warns of a global food crisis. The Pentagon has spent over $15 billion. Iran's new Supreme Leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, is a hardliner who vows to fight on. The world watches — and waits.

Western Hemisphere

The Move
No One Saw Coming:
Maduro's Capture

In January 2026, the United States executed a covert operation that stunned the world — seizing Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. Trump declared America would "take back" Venezuela's oil reserves, the largest proven reserves on Earth.

The move wasn't just about Venezuela. It was a message to Iran, Russia, and China: no alliance with America's enemies is safe. It severed Iran's most important economic lifeline — a shadow network of oil tankers, drone shipments, and sanctioned finance.

2020–2025 Venezuela & Iran build shadow fleet to bypass US sanctions — trading oil, drones, and technology. The MV Bella 1 ("Marinera") becomes the symbol of this axis.
Jan 2026 US forces capture Maduro. MV Bella 1 seized with UK support. Trump calls it a blow to Hezbollah's Latin American drug trafficking income.
Feb 2026 Iran-Venezuela economic corridor collapses. Iran loses key oil revenue at the worst possible moment — just before Operation Epic Fury begins.
Mar 2026 Analysts compare Venezuela operation to Iran war — noting the swift, clean removal of Maduro versus the grinding, costly war of attrition with Tehran.
The Fallout

What's at Stake
for the World

01 ⚡

The Energy Crisis

The Strait of Hormuz — through which 19% of global LNG and massive oil flows — is effectively closed. Brent crude has surpassed $100/barrel. Energy prices are surging across Europe, Asia, and the developing world.

02 🌾

The Food Security Threat

50% of global urea and sulfur exports — critical fertilizers — transit the Strait. The UN World Food Programme warns of a global food crisis echoing 2022. Rising fertilizer prices mean rising food prices everywhere on Earth.

03 🏛️

Cultural Heritage Destroyed

UNESCO World Heritage Sites have been damaged in the strikes. The ancient Golestan Palace — a monument of Persian civilization — was struck on March 2. The Azadi Sport Complex was bombed on March 5. History is burning.

04 ✈️

Global Aviation Disruption

Iran's airspace is closed. Bahrain, Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, Syria, and the UAE have closed their airspace. Hundreds of international flights are being rerouted daily, adding costs and hours to journeys across the globe.

05 💰

The Economic Shockwave

The World Bank had already projected Iran's economy shrinking through 2026. Now the global economy faces inflationary pressure, slowed growth, and supply chain disruption on a scale not seen since the COVID-19 pandemic.

06 ⚖️

The Legal & Moral Reckoning

The International Commission of Jurists has called the Israeli-US strikes a violation of international law. UNESCO has issued statements of deep concern. Russia and most of the Global South have condemned the campaign. The world is divided.

Ripple Effects

The War
Beyond the War

I
Russia & China Watch Closely

Russia has condemned the strikes as a violation of international law. China — the world's largest crude oil importer at a record 11.6M barrels/day in 2025 — faces severe supply disruption and is stockpiling aggressively.

II
The Gulf States Caught in the Middle

Saudi Arabia, which lobbied Trump to attack Iran, now faces Iranian missile strikes on its own soil. The Gulf monarchies are simultaneously allies of the US offensive and targets of Iranian retaliation.

III
Air Defense Running Low

Iran's strategy is calculated: force the depletion of US and Israeli ballistic missile interceptors. Once those stocks run low — the calculus of the entire war shifts dramatically.

IV
Diplomacy's Window Is Closing

The International Crisis Group calls for an immediate ceasefire. But with Khamenei dead and a hardliner in his place, Iran has no face-saving path to a deal — and the US has offered none.
